Fusarium Wilt

Fusarium wilt is a fungal disease that affects cotton plants. It is caused by the fungus Fusarium oxysporum and is most commonly found in warm, humid climates. Symptoms of Fusarium wilt include yellowing and wilting of the leaves, as well as stunted plant growth

Cotton Curl Virus

Cotton curl virus (CCV) is a viral disease that affects cotton plants. It is transmitted by whiteflies and causes a range of symptoms including curled, distorted leaves, stunted plant growth, and reduced yields. there is no treatment if the plant is once affected by this virus
