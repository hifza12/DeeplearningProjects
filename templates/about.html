About our Approach

Transfer learning is a machine learning technique that involves using a pre-trained model as a starting point for a new task, rather than training a model from scratch. This can be especially useful when there is a limited amount of data available for the new task, as the pre-trained model will already have learned many of the general features and patterns present in the data.

In the context of cotton plant disease prediction, transfer learning could be used to train a deep learning model to classify images of healthy and diseased cotton plants. For example, you could use a pre-trained model such as ResNet or VGG that has been trained on a large dataset of general images, and then fine-tune the model on a smaller dataset of cotton plant images.

It is important to note that transfer learning is not a one-size-fits-all solution, and the specific approach you take will depend on the size and complexity of your dataset and the specific requirements of your task
